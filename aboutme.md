### A Note on How I See Life

I believe that death is certain,  
and that once it arrives, it no longer belongs to the one who has died—  
because there is no longer a subject who can feel it.  
Its weight falls entirely on those who are still alive.

Because the end exists, the present matters.  
Because time is limited, choice cannot be postponed.

Existence comes before essence.  
I am not defined in advance by a title or expectation,  
but shaped over time by the decisions I make,  
and by what I choose to take responsibility for.

The world does not guarantee meaning.  
In that quiet indifference, meaning is not discovered—it is assumed.  
To care, to build, to love, even without assurance,  
is a way of resisting emptiness with intent.

Freedom is not light.  
It comes without excuses and without substitutes.  
And so, I try to live consciously—  
aware that this life is mine to own.
